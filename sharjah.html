Sharjah

Sharjah, the third-largest emirate in the United Arab Emirates, encompasses an area of approximately 2,590 square kilometers. It is celebrated for its rich cultural heritage, often referred to as the "Cultural Capital of the Arab World." Sharjah's history is deeply rooted in the preservation of Arabic culture, arts, and traditions.

The ruler of Sharjah, Sheikh Sultan bin Muhammad Al Qasimi, has been a prominent advocate for culture and education. His reign has seen the establishment of numerous museums, galleries, and cultural festivals, making Sharjah a haven for artists, historians, and literature enthusiasts. The emirate's commitment to cultural endeavors is further exemplified by its recognition as the UNESCO World Book Capital in 2019.

Sharjah is also known for its stunning architecture, including the Al Noor Mosque and the Heart of Sharjah, a historic district where visitors can experience the emirate's traditional charm. Beyond culture, Sharjah has made significant strides in education, with several universities and institutions fostering academic excellence.

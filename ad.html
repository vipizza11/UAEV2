Abu Dhabi

Abu Dhabi, the capital of the United Arab Emirates, is the largest emirate, covering an extensive area of approximately 67,340 square kilometers. It has a rich history as a vital center for trade, pearl diving, and Bedouin culture long before the discovery of oil in the mid-20th century. The emirate has grown to be a modern metropolis known for its stunning architecture, vibrant culture, and luxurious lifestyle.



Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan, who has been the ruler of Abu Dhabi since 2004, also serves as the President of the United Arab Emirates. Under his leadership, Abu Dhabi has experienced significant economic growth and development, becoming a global hub for finance, tourism, and commerce while preserving its rich heritage. The emirate is home to cultural landmarks such as the Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque and ambitious projects like Saadiyat Island, which hosts world-class museums and cultural institutions. The presence of oil wealth has allowed Abu Dhabi to invest in diversifying its economy, with a strong focus on sustainable development and renewable energy, making it a dynamic and forward-looking emirate.
